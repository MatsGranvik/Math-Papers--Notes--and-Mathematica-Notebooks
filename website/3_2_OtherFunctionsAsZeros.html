Okay, we've examined, extensively, this sampling error that visually coincides with the Riemann Zeta function. And we've noted special amplitude/frequencies pairs where the sampling error goes away.
Thus far, I've shown the sampling error interesting and unpredictable, but not why people care about it (other than the cash prizes, of course).
My aim in this section is to change that.
Here's the plan of attack. First, I'll provide a refresher on polynomials expressed as products of their zeroes. I will then assert the surprising fact that the Riemann Zeta function can likewise be expressed as a product of its infinite number of zeroes. I'l then show how those zeros can be used to describe the sampling error in another interesting discrete-to-continuous sampling problem.
